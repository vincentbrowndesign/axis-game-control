# Construction Zones

Construction Zone means a player is actively working through a correction.

During this period, the coach prioritizes mechanical compliance over short-term results.

## Statuses

Active:

- The correction is being introduced.
- The player may regress while changing the habit.
- The coach should keep the cue simple and repeatable.

Stabilizing:

- The player can show the correction in controlled or guided pressure.
- The coach should test it with more realistic timing and spacing.
- The trigger should still be used.

Cleared:

- The correction is stable enough to leave Construction Zone.
- The coach can still retrieve the clip if the flaw returns.

## Use Cases

Use Construction Zone for:

- Footwork changes.
- Screen angles.
- Catch stance.
- Closeout behavior.
- Help rotations.
- Passing decisions under pressure.

Do not use it as a score, grade, or player label.

Construction Zone is temporary protection for adaptation.
