# Basketball Tech Roadmap

Axis is not trying to copy basketball hardware companies.

Hardware companies track events.

Axis remembers behavior.

Stats tell what happened.

Axis builds continuity.

## 1. Current Basketball Tech Categories

### Shot Tracking

Shot systems measure makes, misses, arc, depth, left-right error, release speed, and court location.

### Wearables

Wearables measure physiology and load: heart rate, acceleration, jump load, readiness, recovery, and movement volume.

### Pose Tracking

Pose systems estimate body landmarks, joint movement, stance, limb paths, and movement rhythm.

### Smart Balls

Smart balls can measure dribble cadence, spin, release speed, touch, and ball movement signatures.

### AI Cameras

AI cameras follow play, isolate clips, identify activity windows, and automate capture without manual camera work.

### VR / Simulation

Simulation tools rehearse decisions, spatial reads, timing, and scenario repetition.

### Smart Courts

Smart courts combine sensors, cameras, location data, and event capture into a full-court signal environment.

### Footwear / Body Load

Footwear and body-load tools estimate impact, acceleration, cutting volume, jump volume, and lower-body stress.

## 2. What Axis Borrows

Shot tracking teaches Axis that repeated context matters more than one isolated clip.

Wearables teach Axis that body signals can become memory context when attached to a session.

Pose tracking teaches Axis to observe geometry, rhythm, and persistence before making basketball claims.

Smart balls teach Axis that cadence and repetition are useful behavioral primitives.

AI cameras teach Axis that capture should feel automatic and directional.

VR teaches Axis that repetition and scenario continuity can build a useful player model.

Smart courts teach Axis that many signals become more valuable when they share one identity anchor.

Footwear and body-load systems teach Axis to treat movement load as context, not diagnosis.
