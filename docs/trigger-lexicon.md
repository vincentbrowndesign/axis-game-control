# Trigger Lexicon

Triggers are compressed coaching cues.

They should be short enough to use during practice and specific enough to connect back to the saved correction.

## Rules

- One or two words max.
- Use basketball language.
- Tie each trigger to a specific correction.
- Avoid motivational slogans.
- Avoid vague commands.

## Core Triggers

TIGHT:

- Shave the screen.
- Reduce space around the screener.
- Do not round the route.

SINK:

- Drop hips.
- Get low before the catch.
- Prepare to play through contact.

LOW:

- Stay in stance.
- Keep the ball protected.
- Win the first step.

HIT:

- Make contact before releasing.
- Finish the screen, box out, or tag.

ICE:

- Keep the ball out of the middle.
- Angle the handler toward the sideline.

HOLD:

- Keep the dribble alive.
- Wait for help to commit.

KICK:

- Move the ball to the open teammate.
- Punish the help rotation.

TAG:

- Get body on the roller.
- Delay the roll before recovery.

LIFT:

- Move out of the corner.
- Create the passing window.

NEXT:

- Make the next pass.
- Do not hold the advantage.
